The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild

"No kingdom. No memories. After a 100-year slumber, Link wakes up alone in a world he no longer remembers. Now the legendary hero must explore a vast and dangerous land and regain his memories before Hyrule is lost forever."
















The Importance of Breath of the Wild

This game was the first open-world Zelda game. A brand new map, infinite things to discover, infinite ways to complete a game. Run, swim, jump, climb, and sail your way around through mountains, rivers, deserts, and a volcano to save the world and rescue the princess. There is no right or wrong way to explore or complete the game. The world is so open and flexible that you could start the game and almost immediately make your way to the final boss if you so desired. You learn to cook over 100 recipes and meet 900 Koroks (forest dwellers). There are no identical paths to upgrading Link's abilities and helping the people of Hyrule. This comes after major complaints of narrow, fully linerar games in the series. It is incredibly difficult to find opponents of this game, simply because it addressed and fixed all the fan critiques from previous games.
